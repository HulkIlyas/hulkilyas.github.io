Overview

TL;DR — idarr does not collect, process, store, or transmit any personal data. This policy exists to be transparent about that fact, and to comply with app store requirements.

This Privacy Policy describes how idarr ("the app", "we", "our") handles information in connection with your use of the idarr application, available on the Google Play Store.

By downloading or using idarr, you agree to the terms outlined in this policy. If you do not agree, please discontinue use of the app.

This policy applies exclusively to the idarr mobile application. It does not apply to any other websites, services, or applications that may link to the app or that the app may link to.

03

How the app works locally

idarr runs entirely on your device. Any content you create, configure, or interact with within the app is stored locally in the app's private sandbox on your device's internal storage — it is never uploaded, synced, or shared with any server.

Local storage

idarr may use Android's standard local storage mechanisms (such as SharedPreferences or an SQLite database) to persist your in-app preferences and data between sessions. This data lives exclusively on your device. It is erased when you uninstall the app.

Network connectivity

idarr does not establish any network connections to external servers for the purpose of transmitting personal data. If the app has network connectivity at all, it is solely to serve a core feature that is clearly visible to you within the app — never silently.

Note — The absence of analytics or crash reporting means we genuinely do not receive feedback when something goes wrong. If you encounter a bug, please contact us directly so we can fix it.

04

Android permissions

Android requires apps to declare permissions they may need. Any permission listed in idarr's manifest is used solely to power a specific, user-facing feature — never to collect data. The current version of idarr requests the minimum set of permissions needed to function.

You can review and revoke permissions at any time via Settings → Apps → idarr → Permissions on your Android device. Revoking a permission may disable related features, but will never affect data privacy — because no data is transmitted regardless.

Important — If a future version of idarr requests a sensitive permission not currently in use (such as camera or microphone), this policy will be updated and a specific justification will be added here before that release is published.

Advertising

idarr contains no advertisements. There are no banner ads, interstitial ads, rewarded ads, or native ads of any kind. No advertising network is integrated. No advertising identifier (GAID) is read or transmitted.

idarr is a free app with no monetization through advertising — current or planned. Should that change in a future version, this policy will be updated, and you will be notified through the app's Play Store release notes before any update containing ads is published.

Current status — idarr is entirely ad-free. No ad SDK, no tracking, no behavioural profiling.

08

Data retention

Since idarr collects no personal data, there is nothing for us to retain or delete on our side. All data created by you within the app (preferences, user-generated content) resides solely on your device.

To delete all app data:

— Uninstall the app — all app data is removed from your device automatically by Android.
— Or go to Settings → Apps → idarr → Storage → Clear Data to wipe app data without uninstalling.

After uninstalling idarr, no residual data about you exists anywhere — neither on your device nor on our servers (we have no servers).

09

Security

The most effective data security is to not collect data in the first place. idarr is designed around this principle.

Local data stored by the app on your device is protected by Android's application sandbox — other apps cannot access it without root access. For additional security, we recommend keeping your device's operating system up to date and enabling device-level encryption (enabled by default on modern Android devices).

We cannot guarantee the absolute security of data on your physical device — we have no control over physical theft, malware, or rooted devices. Use standard Android security practices (screen lock, Play Protect, updated OS) to protect your device generally.

Responsible disclosure — If you discover a security vulnerability in idarr, please report it to us at contact@idarr.app before disclosing it publicly. We take all reports seriously and will respond promptly.

Children's privacy

idarr does not knowingly collect any personal information from children under the age of 13, or under any higher applicable age threshold in their jurisdiction (e.g. 16 under GDPR in certain countries).

Because idarr collects no personal data from anyone, it is inherently compliant with:

— COPPA (Children's Online Privacy Protection Act, USA)
— Google Play Families Policy
— GDPR Article 8 (processing of children's data)
— UK GDPR and the Age Appropriate Design Code

If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has somehow submitted personal information through the app, please contact us. We will promptly investigate and address your concern — although given the app's architecture, we do not believe this is technically possible.
